Edmund Hillary

The man who conquered the worlds highest mountian

Sir Edmund and his climbing companion Tenzing Norgay at the summit of Everest

  • 1919 On July 20, 1919, Edmund Percival Hillary was born in Auckland, New Zealand. His father was Percival Augustus Hillary; he went by "Percy." His mother was Gertrude Hillary (maiden name Clark).
  • 1920 Hillary’s family moved to Tuakau, a smaller town south of Auckland. Edmund's father had been allocated eight acres of land as a returned soldier of the 15th Regiment.
  • 1935 At the age of 16, Hillary went on school trip to Mount Ruapehu and is exposed to climbing for the first time. He is quoted as saying that he "wanted to see the world." He went on to attend Auckland University College, but dropped out after two years.
  • 1939 Hillary completed his first major climb. He reached the peak of Mount Ollivier in the Canterbury Region of New Zealand with his friend Tenzing Norgay. The summit of Mount Ollivier has an elevation of 6,342 feet (1,933 meters).
  • 1948 Hillary climbed to the south ridge of Aoraki to Mount Cook, New Zealand’s highest peak. The official height is currently listed as 12,218 feet (3,724 meters), but the South Peak is only 11,788 feet (3,593 meters).
  • 1949 Hillary went to Switzerland to climb in the Swiss Alps. It was there that he ascended Jungfrau (4,158 meters or 13,642 feet).
  • 1951 Hillary participated in a reconnaissance expedition to Mount Everest.
  • 1952 Hillary was part of the British team that tried unsuccessfully to climb the Cho Oyu, the sixth highest mountain in the world at 26,864 feet above sea level.
  • 1953 On May 29, Hillary and Norgay became the first climbers to reach the summit of Mount Everest. It was this same year that Hillary was knighted by Queen Elizabeth and became Sir Edmund Hillary. He also married Louise Mary Rose in 1953.
